## Overview

Advanced functional equations often mix algebra and number theory. Expect to
find hidden constraints via special inputs and symmetry.

## Key Ideas

- If $f$ is defined on integers, parity and modular arguments can restrict it.
- Look for fixed points: solve $f(x)=x$ or $f(x)=c$.
- Iteration (apply the equation repeatedly) can uncover structure.

## Core Skills

### Pin Down Constants

Use $x=0$, $y=0$, $x=y$, or $x=-y$ to extract $f(0)$ and other constants.

### Iterate the Functional Equation

Apply the equation multiple times to relate $f(x)$ to $f(f(x))$ or $f(x+c)$.

### Use Injectivity/Surjectivity

Prove injectivity by showing $f(a)=f(b)\Rightarrow a=b$, then use it to
simplify equations.

## Worked Example

If $f(x)=2x$ satisfies $f(x+y)=f(x)+f(y)$ for all $x,y$, then $f$ is additive.
Many contest problems ask you to prove that linear functions are the only
solutions.

## Easy Warm-up Examples

### Example 1: Fixed Point

If $f(f(x))=x$, show $f$ is bijective.

Apply $f$ to both sides to get $f(x)=f(f(f(x)))$, implying surjectivity and
injectivity.

### Example 2: Integer Restriction

If $f:\mathbb{Z}\to\mathbb{Z}$ and $f(x+1)=f(x)+2$, then $f(x)=2x+c$.

### Example 3: Symmetry

If $f(x)+f(1-x)=1$, then setting $x=1-x$ gives $f(1/2)=1/2$.
### Example 4: Injectivity Practice

If

$$
f(x+f(y))=f(x)+y
$$

for all real numbers $x,y$, your goal is to show that $f$ is injective.

Suppose $f(a)=f(b)$. Then

$$
f(x+f(a))=f(x)+a,
$$

and

$$
f(x+f(b))=f(x)+b.
$$

Since $f(a)=f(b)$, we obtain $a=b$.

### Example 5: Surjectivity Practice

If

$$
f(f(x))=f(0)+x
$$

for all real numbers $x$, your job is to show that $f$ is surjective.

As $x$ varies over $\mathbb{R}$, the right-hand side attains every real value.
Hence $f(f(x))$ attains every real value, so $f$ is surjective.

### Example 6: What c makes the function 0?

Suppose

$$
f(x+f(y))=f(x)+y
$$

for all real numbers $x,y$.

Since $f$ is surjective, choose $c$ such that $f(c)=0$. Then

$$
f(x)=f(x+f(c))=f(x)+c,
$$

so $c=0$.

### Example 7: Finding Solutions

Your job is to find all linear solutions to the following functional equation:

$$
f(x+f(y))=f(x)+2y.
$$

First, we let $f(x)=ax+b$.Then, substituting gives

$$
a(x+ay+b)+b=ax+2y+b.
$$

Comparing coefficients yields

$$
a^2=2,\qquad ab=0.
$$

Since $a\neq 0$, we get $b=0$, so

$$
f(x)=\sqrt{2}\,x
\quad\text{or}\quad
f(x)=-\sqrt{2}\,x.
$$

### Example 8: What is $f(0)$?

Suppose that

$$
f(x+y)+f(x-y)=2f(x)+2f(y)
$$

for all real numbers $x,y$.

Setting $x=y=0$ gives

$$
2f(0)=4f(0),
$$

hence

$$
f(0)=0.
$$

### Example 9: Find the Functional Relation

Suppose

$$
f(x+y)+f(x-y)=2f(x)+2f(y)
$$

and $f(0)=0$.

Setting $y=x$ gives

$$
f(2x)+f(0)=4f(x),
$$

so

$$
f(2x)=4f(x).
$$

### Example 10: Iteration

If $f(f(x))=f(0)+x$, show that $f(f(f(f(x))))=2f(0)+x$.

Applying the given relation with $x$ replaced by $f(f(x))$ gives

$$f(f(f(f(x))))=f(0)+f(f(x)).$$

Using the original equation again,

$$f(f(f(f(x))))=f(0)+(f(0)+x)=2f(0)+x.$$
### Example 11: Constant Solutions

Your goal here is to find all constant solutions of

$$
f(x+y)=f(x)f(y)-f(xy)+1.
$$

Let $f(x)\equiv c$. Then

$$
c=c^2-c+1.
$$

Thus

$$
(c-1)^2=0,
$$

so the only constant solution is

$$
f(x)\equiv 1.
$$

### Example 12: What is $f(0)$?

If

$$
f(x+y)=f(x)f(y)-f(xy)+1
$$

for all real numbers $x,y$, show that either $f(0)=1$ or $f$ is constant.

Setting $x=0$ gives

$$
f(y)=f(0)f(y)-f(0)+1.
$$

Letting $a=f(0)$, we obtain

$$
(1-a)f(y)=1-a.
$$

Thus either $a=1$ or

$$
f(y)\equiv 1.
$$
